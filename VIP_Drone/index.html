Urban Air Mobility VIP

About

In my first year, I was part of a VIP(Vertically Integrated Project) for Urban Air Mobility,
where my team was responsible for developing test-bed drones that are able to run experimental code for urban flight path generation. This research was my first exposure to many more complex drone components such as the Pixhawk flight controller. I was grateful to be on a team with a few experienced upperclass students who showed me how to work with this hardware, and I was glad to be able to contribute by learning as fast as possible and with my previous knowledge of computer networking and basic drone flight controls. I gained valuable experience working on an engineering project with a team as well as with more professional resources such as the IFL(Indoor Flight Laboratory) where we gathered data on the performance of the drones.
